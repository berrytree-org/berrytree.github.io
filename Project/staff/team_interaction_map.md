## **Interaction Layers**

1. **Function & Logic Layer**

   * **Kate → Terry → Malcolm → Sherlock**
   * Kate provides data and functionality; Terry ensures implementation; Malcolm validates quality; Sherlock checks security and privacy.

2. **Design & Experience Layer**

   * **Vincent ↔ Lila ↔ Terry ↔ Malcolm**
   * Vincent enhances feel and polish; Lila ensures structural feasibility; Terry validates; Malcolm enforces quality standards.

3. **Narrative & Content Layer**

   * **Andrew ↔ Kate & Tom ↔ Martha**
   * Andrew crafts stories and SEO-friendly content; Kate and Tom supply structured and clean data; Martha ensures genealogical and factual accuracy.

4. **Coordination & Wellbeing Layer**

   * **Pam ↔ Rebecca ↔ All Team Members**
   * Pam tracks tasks, timelines, and feedback; Rebecca monitors team morale and resolves conflicts; both ensure efficient collaboration.

## **Decision Flow Example**

1. Kate deploys a new interactive feature.
2. Terry tests functionality and cross-browser compatibility.
3. Malcolm reviews against quality standards.
4. Sherlock evaluates security and privacy risks.
5. Vincent adds micro-interactions and visual refinements.
6. Lila integrates the polished feature into the site.
7. Andrew writes narrative and SEO content referencing Tom’s mapped data.
8. Martha verifies content accuracy and source integrity.
9. Pam coordinates tasks, feedback, and ensures deadlines are met.
10. Rebecca checks in on team energy and engagement.

Outcome: **Feature ships functional, polished, secure, accurate, and narratively engaging.**
