## The Story of David J. Berry

David J. Berry was the son of Robert and Elizabeth Cate Berry of Orange County, North Carolina. He was born around 1774 and spent his early years on the family land in Orange County, where Berrys had lived since his grandfather Robert and Mary Williamson Berry first settled from Princess Anne County, Virginia.

On February 21, 1797, David married **Mary Blalock**. Mary came into the marriage with a young daughter, Elizabeth Leathers Berry, born around 1791. A bastardy bond had been issued against John Leathers in Orange County on August 23, 1791 ([view the transcript](/images/maryblalockbond.jpg)). David and Mary raised Elizabeth as their own child.

David and Mary's first child together was likely **Sarah "Sally" Berry**, born about April 4, 1796, who married William A. Cates in Orange County on February 26, 1817. Their daughter **Winifred Berry** married Joshua Norman on September 19, 1820, also in North Carolina. The rest of their children married in Georgia after the family moved south.

### Life in Orange County

The family lived in Orange County, North Carolina, through at least 1824. Their youngest child, **Joshua M. Berry**, was born there on April 13, 1824, all of David and Mary's children were born in North Carolina. David appeared in the Orange County census in 1800, 1810, and 1820.

In September 1821, David and his brother Henry sold a slave girl in Person County. David had inherited a slave but none of his father's land — the plantation was willed to his younger brother Henry Berry in 1814. David was the last of Robert and Elizabeth Cate Berry's children to leave home.

### The Move to Georgia

Sometime between 1825 and 1830, David J. Berry and the rest of his family removed to **Cobb County, Georgia**. By then, some of his family were already there — William A. Cates and his wife Sarah Berry Cates had their third child born in South Carolina in 1822, suggesting the family moved in stages.

In 1821, bounty land was being made available after the Creek Indians ceded their land to the state. David may have settled in **Fayette County, Georgia**, near where the children of his sister Mary Berry Camp lived.

On November 30, 1858, the State of Georgia took part of Fayette County and Henry County to form **Clayton County**. David's youngest son, Joshua M. Berry, lived and died in Jonesboro, Clayton County.

### A Family Tragedy

Joshua M. Berry and his wife Elizabeth Cates Berry had two sons killed at a railroad crossing in Jonesboro, Georgia, on **October 20, 1921**. Robert I. Berry (age 53) and Joshua Berry Jr. (age 54) were taking a load of produce from their farm into town on their truck when they were struck by a train as they attempted to cross the railroad tracks. The two brothers were well respected in the community. They were not killed instantly but both died a short time later in the hospital.
