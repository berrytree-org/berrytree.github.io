## The Story of Henry Berry

Henry Berry was born in 1776 in Orange County, North Carolina, the youngest of the ten children of Robert and Elizabeth Cate Berry. He had a daughter by **Fanny Ashley**, though they were never married. This is known because Fanny Ashley was living with her daughter Mary Ashley Berry King in 1850.

### Inheriting the Plantation

Henry was the one who inherited the **Fiddleton Plantation** from his father Robert Berry. Robert's will, written in 1812, left the plantation and its land to Henry — making him the last of the family to remain on the original Berry homestead in Orange County.

In September 1821, Henry and his brother David sold a slave girl in Person County.

On November 23, 1830, Henry sold 100 acres to his nephews **William Clarence Berry** and **Thomas Person Berry** (sons of his brother William). The land sale is recorded in Orange County Deed Book 24, Page 226.

In 1850, Henry was living at the Fiddleton Plantation with **Hannah Cate Berry** (his sister-in-law, wife of his brother William who had abandoned her). They were living next to Thomas Person Berry and his family.

Henry left the plantation home he had inherited to his daughter **Mary Ashley Berry** and her husband **Thomas King** in 1855.
