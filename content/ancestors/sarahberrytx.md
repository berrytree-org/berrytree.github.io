{{< img-float src="/images/sberrysmalltx.jpg" alt="Portrait of Sarah Berry" side="left" >}}She was born on April 20, 1839 in Alabama and died in {{< img-float src="/images/sarahtxhs.jpg" alt="Sarah Berry Dippold headstone" side="right" >}} Galveston Texas on June 5th 1917. We were able to find Sarah Berry Dippold's grave in 2006 while I was staying in Houston for medical reasons. The grave is located in the Trinity Episcopal Church Cemetery which is located in Galveston Texas. She is buried in Block # 100 plot 18 of that Cemetery. I was unable to locate her husband but he may still be buried there. This Cemetery has been raised several times, as it keeps sinking they haul in more dirt. Each family is responsible to see that their relatives headstones are {{< img-float src="/images/sarah-berry-dippold-grave.jpg" alt="Sarah Berry Dippold grave marker in Trinity Episcopal Church Cemetery, Galveston" >}}raised to the new level. If the family does not assume that responsibility the grave is covered with dirt and is no longer visible. The sinking of the Island is caused by removal of oil from the surface below. Sarah was married five times. This was during the period of the civil war and may be the reason so many of them died. She married her first husband Hiram Howard on November 24th 1853. {{< img-float src="/images/hdippoldstonetx.jpg" alt="Henrick Dippold headstone" side="right" >}} Sarah married her second husband, Francis M. McClure on January 5th 1859. On May 18th 1863 Sarah married her third husband Robert A. Taylor. Sarah married her fourth husband, Henry B. Stoneham on August 29 1868. She married her fifth husband Henrick Dippold on February 15th 1880.

There were no children born to the last two marriages.
